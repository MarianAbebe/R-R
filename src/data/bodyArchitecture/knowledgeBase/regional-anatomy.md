# Regional Anatomy

## Learning Objectives

- Identify major surface regions of the head, trunk, upper limb, and lower limb.
- Connect regional terms to underlying bones, joints, and muscles.
- Use regional anatomy language in clinical-style descriptions.

## Key Concepts

- Cephalic: head; cervical: neck; thoracic: chest.
- Lumbar: lower back; sacral: posterior pelvis.
- Acromial: shoulder point; brachial: arm; antebrachial: forearm; carpal: wrist.
- Femoral: thigh; crural: leg; tarsal: ankle; plantar: sole.

## High-Yield Facts

- Antecubital is the anterior elbow.
- Olecranal is the posterior elbow.
- Popliteal is the posterior knee.
- Patellar is the anterior knee.
- Inguinal is the groin region.

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing carpal and tarsal.
- Calling the patellar region the sole of the foot.
- Confusing antecubital and olecranal.
- Using abdominal and thoracic regions interchangeably.

## Application Examples

- Pain in the groin crease is described as inguinal.
- The posterior knee is the popliteal region.
- The cheek is the buccal region.
- The chin is the mental region.

## Oral Exam Style Prompts

- Identify the region at the front of the elbow.
- Identify the region at the back of the knee.
- Move from shoulder to wrist using regional terms.
- Move from thigh to ankle using regional terms.
