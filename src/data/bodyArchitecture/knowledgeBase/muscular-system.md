# Muscular System

## Learning Objectives

- Distinguish skeletal, cardiac, and smooth muscle.
- Explain agonist, antagonist, synergist, and fixator roles.
- Identify high-yield muscles and actions in the limbs, trunk, and head and neck.

## Key Concepts

- Skeletal muscle is voluntary and usually moves bones.
- Cardiac muscle forms the myocardium.
- Smooth muscle is found in many hollow organs and vessels.
- Tendons attach muscle to bone.
- Origin is usually more fixed; insertion usually moves more.

## High-Yield Facts

- Deltoid abducts the shoulder.
- Biceps brachii flexes the elbow and supinates the forearm.
- Triceps brachii extends the elbow.
- Quadriceps extend the knee.
- Hamstrings flex the knee and extend the hip.
- Diaphragm is the primary muscle of inspiration.

## Common Mistakes

- Confusing ligaments with tendons.
- Calling a fixator the prime mover.
- Saying tibialis anterior plantarflexes the ankle.
- Misidentifying biceps femoris as an upper limb muscle.

## Application Examples

- Foot drop involves impaired dorsiflexion, often linked to anterior leg compartment function.
- Scapular winging can involve serratus anterior dysfunction.
- Rising from a chair requires powerful hip extension by gluteus maximus.
- Sit-ups emphasize trunk flexion by rectus abdominis.

## Oral Exam Style Prompts

- Define agonist and antagonist with an elbow example.
- Explain origin versus insertion.
- Name the major muscles responsible for knee extension and knee flexion.
- Describe the role of the diaphragm during inspiration.
