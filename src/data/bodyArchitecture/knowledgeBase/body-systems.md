# Body Systems

## Learning Objectives

- Summarize the major functions of each body system.
- Connect organs to their systems and cavities.
- Explain simple system integration examples.

## Key Concepts

- Cardiovascular system transports blood through heart and vessels.
- Respiratory system conducts air and exchanges gases.
- Digestive system breaks down food and absorbs nutrients.
- Urinary system filters blood and regulates fluid balance.
- Nervous and endocrine systems coordinate body functions.
- Lymphatic system returns fluid and supports immune defense.

## High-Yield Facts

- Capillaries are the main exchange vessels.
- Alveoli are the main gas exchange surfaces.
- Small intestine is the major site of nutrient absorption.
- Kidneys are retroperitoneal and produce urine.
- Endocrine glands release hormones into blood.

## Common Mistakes

- Saying arteries always carry oxygenated blood.
- Confusing ureter and urethra.
- Assigning bile production to the gallbladder instead of the liver.
- Calling lymphatic vessels airways.

## Application Examples

- Exercise integrates muscular, respiratory, and cardiovascular systems.
- Hormone signaling uses the endocrine system and blood transport.
- Fluid balance involves urinary, cardiovascular, and endocrine control.
- Infection responses can involve lymph nodes and lymphocytes.

## Oral Exam Style Prompts

- Trace oxygen from inhalation to tissue delivery.
- Trace urine from kidney to outside the body.
- Explain how the digestive and cardiovascular systems cooperate after a meal.
- Compare nervous and endocrine control.
