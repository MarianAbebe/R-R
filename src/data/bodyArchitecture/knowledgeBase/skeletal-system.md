# Skeletal System

## Learning Objectives

- Identify axial and appendicular skeletal components.
- Describe major bone functions and bone cell roles.
- Recognize high-yield bones of the skull, vertebral column, thoracic cage, and limbs.

## Key Concepts

- Axial skeleton: skull, vertebral column, ribs, and sternum.
- Appendicular skeleton: limb bones plus pectoral and pelvic girdles.
- Bone cells: osteoblasts build bone, osteoclasts resorb bone, osteocytes maintain bone.
- Bone categories include long, short, flat, irregular, and sesamoid bones.

## High-Yield Facts

- The patella is a sesamoid bone.
- The diaphysis is the shaft of a long bone.
- Red bone marrow supports hematopoiesis.
- The acetabulum receives the head of the femur.
- The foramen magnum is in the occipital bone.

## Common Mistakes

- Calling the humerus a forearm bone.
- Confusing carpals with tarsals.
- Saying ribs 11 and 12 are true ribs.
- Mixing up osteoblast and osteoclast functions.

## Application Examples

- A fall on an outstretched hand may injure the scaphoid.
- The olecranon forms the posterior point of the elbow.
- The tibia is the medial weight-bearing leg bone.
- The fibula forms the lateral malleolus.

## Oral Exam Style Prompts

- Divide the skeleton into axial and appendicular components.
- Explain the difference between compact and spongy bone.
- Identify the major bones of the upper limb from proximal to distal.
- Identify the major bones of the lower limb from proximal to distal.
