# Joints

## Learning Objectives

- Classify joints structurally and functionally.
- Identify common synovial joint types and examples.
- Apply movement terms to upper and lower limb actions.

## Key Concepts

- Structural classes: fibrous, cartilaginous, and synovial joints.
- Functional classes: synarthrosis, amphiarthrosis, and diarthrosis.
- Synovial joints have a cavity, articular cartilage, capsule, synovial membrane, and synovial fluid.
- Synovial types include plane, hinge, pivot, condylar, saddle, and ball-and-socket.

## High-Yield Facts

- Shoulder and hip are ball-and-socket joints.
- Elbow is mainly a hinge joint.
- Proximal radioulnar joint is a pivot joint.
- Thumb carpometacarpal joint is a saddle joint.
- Knee menisci improve congruence and distribute load.

## Common Mistakes

- Calling the knee a suture.
- Confusing pronation/supination with ankle movements.
- Saying dorsiflexion points the toes downward.
- Assuming all joints are synovial.

## Application Examples

- Flexion decreases joint angle; extension increases it.
- Abduction moves a limb away from the midline.
- Inversion turns the sole medially.
- Plantarflexion points the foot downward.

## Oral Exam Style Prompts

- Compare fibrous, cartilaginous, and synovial joints.
- Describe the features of a synovial joint.
- Explain the movements possible at the shoulder.
- Explain dorsiflexion, plantarflexion, inversion, and eversion.
