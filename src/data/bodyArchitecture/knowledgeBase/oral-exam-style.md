# Oral Exam Style

## Learning Objectives

- Practice concise anatomy explanations under exam-style pressure.
- Integrate terminology, regions, bones, joints, muscles, and systems.
- Build structured answers that move from broad context to specific detail.

## Key Concepts

- Start with the body region or system.
- Name the structure precisely.
- State the relationship, function, or movement.
- Add one high-yield clinical or anatomical relevance point when useful.

## High-Yield Facts

- Clear directional terms prevent ambiguity.
- Regional terms often anchor the examiner's prompt.
- Bones, joints, and muscles should be linked by action.
- Cavity and membrane relationships are common oral exam targets.

## Common Mistakes

- Giving a correct structure without orientation.
- Mixing patient left/right with observer left/right.
- Naming a muscle without stating its action.
- Naming a joint without classifying or describing its movement.

## Application Examples

- The heart is medial to the lungs and lies in the mediastinum.
- The patellar region is the anterior knee, and knee extension is produced mainly by quadriceps.
- The popliteal region is posterior to the knee, and knee flexion involves hamstrings.
- The diaphragm separates thoracic and abdominal cavities and drives quiet inspiration.

## Oral Exam Style Prompts

- Describe the shoulder joint, including joint type and major movement.
- Explain the path of urine from kidney to outside the body.
- Describe the thoracic cage and its role in protection and breathing.
- Compare the anterior elbow and posterior knee using regional terminology.
